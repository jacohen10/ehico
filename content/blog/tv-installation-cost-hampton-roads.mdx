# How Much Does TV Installation Cost in Hampton Roads?

If you're looking to get a TV professionally mounted in Norfolk, Virginia Beach, Chesapeake, or anywhere in Hampton Roads, you're probably wondering what it'll cost. Here's a straightforward breakdown based on our experience completing thousands of installations across the region.

## Quick Answer

Most residential TV installations in Hampton Roads run **$150–$400**, depending on the wall type, whether you want cables hidden, and how complex the setup is.

## Pricing by Service Type

### Basic TV Wall Mount — $150–$200
This covers mounting your TV on a standard drywall wall with a fixed or tilting bracket. Cables are routed externally using a cable cover. Best for bedrooms, offices, and simple living room setups.

**Includes:**
- TV mounting on drywall
- Bracket installation (customer-supplied or we can provide)
- External cable management
- Level and secure mounting with proper anchors

### Standard Installation with Cable Concealment — $250–$350
The most popular option. We run your cables through the wall for a clean, wire-free look. Ideal for living rooms and spaces where aesthetics matter.

**Includes:**
- Everything in basic installation
- In-wall cable routing
- Power and HDMI run behind the wall
- Clean, professional finish with no visible wires

### Above-Fireplace Installation — $300–$400
Fireplace mounts require special consideration for heat, viewing angle, and often masonry anchoring. We use heat-resistant materials and full-motion mounts to angle the TV downward for comfortable viewing.

**Includes:**
- Masonry or brick anchoring
- Heat management assessment
- Full-motion mount for optimal viewing angle
- In-wall cable concealment

### Complex or Custom Installations — $400+
Multi-TV setups, outdoor installations, stone or concrete walls, ceiling mounts, or installations that require electrical work. We'll provide a custom quote after assessing your space.

## What Affects the Price?

A few factors that can move the price up or down:

- **Wall material** — Drywall is standard. Brick, stone, and concrete require masonry anchors and specialized tools, adding $50–$100.
- **Cable concealment** — Running cables through the wall costs more than external cable covers, but looks significantly better.
- **TV size and weight** — Very large TVs (75"+) may require reinforced mounting or a two-person install.
- **Height and accessibility** — High mounts or difficult-to-reach locations take more time.
- **Additional devices** — Setting up a soundbar, Apple TV, or surround sound system adds to the scope.

## Why Hire a Professional?

It's tempting to DIY a TV mount, but there are real risks:

- **Finding studs** — Missing a stud means your TV could fall. We use professional stud finders and verify every anchor point.
- **Electrical safety** — Running power through walls should be done to code. Improper electrical work is a fire hazard.
- **Wall damage** — One wrong hole in the wrong spot can mean costly drywall repair. We get it right the first time.
- **Heavy lifting** — A 65" TV weighing 50+ pounds needs to be lifted and held precisely while being secured. It's a two-person job.
