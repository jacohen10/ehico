# LED Retrofit vs. New Fixtures: Which Is Right for Your Business?

If your business still runs fluorescent lighting, you already know you should switch to LED. The question is how: retrofit your existing fixtures or replace them entirely? Here's how to decide.

## What's the Difference?

### LED Retrofit
You keep your existing fixture housing and replace the internal components — swap fluorescent tubes for LED tubes or install an LED retrofit kit. The fixture itself stays on the ceiling.

### New LED Fixtures
You remove the old fixture completely and install a purpose-built LED fixture. These are designed from the ground up for LED technology.

## Retrofit: Pros and Cons

**Advantages:**
- **Lower upfront cost** — typically 30–50% less than full replacement
- **Faster installation** — less labor since the fixture housing stays in place
- **Less disruption** — no ceiling work, no patching, no repainting
- **Good for newer fixtures** — if your fixture housing is in good shape, why replace it?

**Drawbacks:**
- **Efficiency ceiling** — retrofits are efficient, but purpose-built LED fixtures are optimized for LED light distribution and heat management
- **Fixture condition matters** — if your fixtures are corroded, yellowed, or damaged, retrofitting a bad housing doesn't make sense
- **Ballast considerations** — some retrofits work with existing ballasts (plug-and-play), others require ballast bypass. Bypassing is more efficient long-term but adds labor cost

## New Fixtures: Pros and Cons

**Advantages:**
- **Maximum efficiency** — purpose-built LED optics and heat sinks deliver the most lumens per watt
- **Modern appearance** — slim, clean profiles that look noticeably better than retrofitted old housings
- **Longer lifespan** — no legacy components (old ballasts, aging housings) to fail
- **Better light quality** — designed for even distribution with less glare

**Drawbacks:**
- **Higher upfront cost** — fixture cost plus more labor for removal and installation
- **More disruption** — ceiling tiles may need adjustment, wiring changes, possible patching
- **Disposal costs** — old fixtures need to be removed and properly disposed of

## When to Retrofit

Retrofit makes the most sense when:

- Your fixture housings are in good condition (less than 15 years old)
- You have a large number of fixtures and need to keep costs down
- You want minimal disruption to business operations
- You're on a tight timeline

## When to Replace

New fixtures make more sense when:

- Your existing fixtures are old, damaged, or visually dated
- You're renovating the space anyway
- You want the best possible efficiency and light quality
- The space is customer-facing and appearance matters (retail, lobbies, restaurants)

## The Numbers

Here's a rough comparison for a typical 20-fixture office:

| | Retrofit | New Fixtures |
|---|---|---|
| Cost per fixture | $80–$150 | $150–$300 |
| Total project cost | $1,600–$3,000 | $3,000–$6,000 |
| Energy savings | 40–50% | 50–60% |
| Payback period | 1–2 years | 2–3 years |
| Expected lifespan | 50,000 hours | 50,000–100,000 hours |

Both options pay for themselves through energy savings. The difference is how fast.
